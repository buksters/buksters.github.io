About

Hello! I'm Ibuki, a Japanese-American designer-developer-researcher who relies on hyphens when describing myself because I dwell in a lot of intersections and wear many hats. My happy place is at the intersection of design, technology, and behavior, and I love experimenting and learning new things in this space! Swipe through the pictures below to find out more about where I'm from, or scroll down to my resume for work and education-related information.

A little more about me:

My first plane ride was when I was 1 year old, when my mother and I immigrated to the US from Japan.

Since then, I've had the privilege to do a lot more traveling. This was taken at the Kerið crater in Iceland.

I grew up on Long Island and went to MIT in Boston (↓ me with my brass rat) so I've lived mostly on the East Coast...

except for when I lived on a ski resort in Utah for six months when classes were virtual due to the pandemic,

and more recently, I lived in Japan for six months - a full circle moment.
