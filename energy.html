Do you know what energy is?

You might hear the term a lot but it's actually a pretty abstract concept.

It can't be seen or touched, since it's not a substance. Instead, its effects can be observed and measured.

Energy is a quantity that measures a physical system's ability to produce change, like the movement of objects, emission of light, or variation of temperature.

There are different types of energy, depending on the physical properties of the system. But all of them fall under two categories: potential and kinetic.

Potential energy is stored energy. For example, gravitational potential energy is the energy an object possesses due to its relative position in a gravitational force field.

In other words, if an object has the ability to fall, it has gravitational potential energy.
